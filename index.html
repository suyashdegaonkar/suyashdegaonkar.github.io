About

I'm Suyash Degaonkar, an MS Electrical Engineering student at USC specializing in VLSI design, physical design, and DFT. I operate at the intersection of silicon physics and system architecture — from transistor-level bit-cell design to full RTL-to-GDSII chip flows.

My internship at Western Semiconductor had me optimizing SRAM layouts for SoC L1/L2 cache, achieving 1.43ns read access times and cutting bank area by ~23%. I've since then executed full custom layouts, physical verification, and automation using SKILL scripting.

Beyond the chip, I'm a maker at heart — with published research, an awarded innovation project, and a co-authored patent on sustainable engineering. I believe good hardware should be fast, small, and reliable.
