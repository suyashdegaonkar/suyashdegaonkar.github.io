About

I'm Suyash Degaonkar, an MS Electrical Engineering student at USC specializing in VLSI design, physical design, and DFT. From transistor-level bit-cell to full RTL-to-GDSII chip flows — I work at every layer of the silicon stack.

At Western Semiconductor I optimized SRAM layouts for SoC L1/L2 cache — achieving 1.43ns read access time and cutting bank area by ~23% through bitline optimization and device physics.

Beyond the chip, I'm a maker — published in Springer, co-authored a patent, and won 10+ national awards. I believe great hardware is fast, dense, and signoff-clean.
